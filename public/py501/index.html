Course Information

PY501 reviews mathematical methods used in physics. The focus is on methods frequently encountered in all areas of physics. Topics include the calculus of variations for both particle and continuum systems; some aspects of ordinary and partial differential equations; linear and tensor algebra; calculus on manifolds; calculus in the complex plane; Fourier series and transforms; steepest descent; asymptotic and convergent series; introduction to groups, smooth groups and basic representation theory with applications to physical systems. The course is focused on the understanding of fundamental mathematical concepts and analytical methods; numerical methods are covered in PY502.

Materials: There are many good reference textbooks for the course -- we will not be following any one precisely. You are not required to purchase any particular book, but you may need to consult appropriate texts depending on your knowledge of the subjects or special interests. The ability to find and assimilate relevant information is an important learning objective of the course, and students are expected to devote a substantial amount of time to independent reading.
